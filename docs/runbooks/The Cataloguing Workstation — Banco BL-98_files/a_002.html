03 The loop, per item 20 on the bench at a time

  1. 1Scan the barcode A hit prefills from supplier data — confirm and move on. A miss means it's an enrichment item; keep going.
  2. 2Photograph it Item under the phone. Snap-&-fill does the rest — it's already live and camera-tested in production.
  3. 3Describe it Pull from the web or from Artemis where it exists; voice-dictate otherwise. Two lines is plenty.
  4. 4Classify it Category, and 18+ or not. Verify the classifier — never re-key it. The age flag is decided at enrich time and goes through the shared seal.
  5. 5Price it Sale price and cost. No cost means margin-blind, so it counts as a gap.
  6. 6Print the label No barcode of its own? The label carries a house code and the item joins the scan sheet.
  7. 7Out-tray The counter ticks. That number is the only progress record that exists — and it cannot lie.

04 One cockpit, two modes /pos/cleanup

?mode=sold

The safety net

Reactive. Only shows products that sold while still half-baked — so nothing a cashier rings in a rush falls through permanently. Busiest first.

?mode=bench

The workbench

Proactive. Shows every unfinished product, sold or not — because the migration is about items nobody has rung up yet. Ordered by category, so a batch is a shelf.

05 The counter what replaces the binder

1,247 / 2,000

One SQL expression defines what "unfinished" means, and it feeds both the list and the count. So the number on this screen can never drift from the work in front of you.

06 The rule why there is no ring binder

The paper is a render of the truth, never the truth.

A hand-filed binder becomes a second source of truth, and it starts rotting the day a price changes. Everything printed here is generated from the database and thrown away: print the catalogue Friday, bin it, print a fresh one next Friday. The only paper that earns permanence is the scan sheet for items with no barcode of their own.
