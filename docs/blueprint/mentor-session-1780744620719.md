### 1. Understand Where the Power Is Lost  
When I first built the poly‑phase **alternating‑current (AC) system**, the dominant complaint was that the energy vanished in the wires. The cure was threefold:

| Real‑world principle | What it means for you |
|----------------------|-----------------------|
| **High voltage, low current** reduces I²R losses. | Check the voltage on your main supply. If your meter or the utility provider allows a higher‑voltage tariff (e.g., 400 V three‑phase instead of single‑phase 230 V), you could halve the current required for the same wattage, thus cutting line loss. |
| **Power factor correction** – I installed massive capacitor banks at Niagara to keep the current in step with the voltage. | Many household appliances (especially those with motors or fluorescent lamps) draw reactive power, which the utility still charges for. A simple, correctly sized **capacitor bank** (often sold as “power‑factor correction units” for homes) can improve your factor from ~0.8 to >0.95, shaving off 5‑10 % of the bill. |
| **Efficient motors** – my induction motor had no brushes, no commutator, and ran cooler and quieter than the DC machines of the day. | Replace any old refrigerator, washing‑machine, or water pump with modern **inverter‑driven AC induction models**. These can be up to 30 % more efficient than the legacy units that still linger in many houses. |

### 2. Capture Free Energy Where Nature Offers It  
When I turned the **Niagara Falls** into a 50‑MW generator, the water itself was the source—no fuel burned, no emissions. Beckenried sits on the shore of **Lake Lucerne**; although the lake is calm, the surrounding streams and the modest elevation change can still be used.

- **Micro‑hydro turbine** – A low‑head turbine (rated 0.5–2 kW) if you have a private waterway or a small drop in your garden can generate enough electricity to offset a portion of your night‑time lighting and electronics. The design I patented in 1911 (the “self‑adjusting turbine”) still informs modern micro‑hydro units.  
- **Wind at altitude** – The Alps channel breezes that can be harvested with a **small vertical‑axis wind turbine** (VAWT). My early experiments with rotary magnetic fields proved that a rotating magnetic field can be induced without a conventional stator, a principle that modern VAWTs exploit for quiet, low‑maintenance operation.

Both options are **real, commercially available** today; they are not the “wireless power” fantasies that later plagued my later Wardenclyffe project.

### 3. Reduce the Demand Before You Generate  

In my laboratory I learned that **the best generator is the one that never has to run**. The same holds for any household bill:

| Action (real, historically proven) | Result |
|-----------------------------------|--------|
| **Replace incandescent bulbs** (the kind I once patented improvements for) with **compact fluorescent lamps (CFLs)** or modern **LEDs**. A 60 W lamp becomes a 9 W equivalent – a 85 % drop in lighting consumption. | Immediate savings of €10‑15 per month. |
| **Insulate windows and doors** – I spent months fighting “leakage” in my own New York rooftop laboratory. Heat loss is invisible but costly. | Reduces heating electricity (or gas) by up to 20 % in winter. |
| **Schedule appliances** – My AC generators ran at constant speed; the load was smoothed by **load‑balancing**. Use timers or smart plugs to run the washing machine, dryer, and dishwasher in the off‑peak window (often cheaper tariffs). | Shifts consumption to a cheaper rate, cutting the bill further. |

### 4. A Word on “Free Electricity”  

During my life I chased **the dream of transmitting power without wires**, culminating in the Wardenclyffe Tower. The tower never produced usable energy for the public, and the dream remains technically out of reach with today’s materials. The path to a truly “free” grid lies not in mythic beams, but in **distributed generation** (micro‑hydro, solar, wind) and **efficient use**.
