  2. Pick a catalog match → it opens the EXISTING product (no duplicate). Tap a row under "Already in your catalog". The new-product modal closes and the existing product's Edit opens instead — so you enrich the one that's there, you don't create a twin. no-dupe

  3. The match score is HONEST. A good hit reads a real % (roughly 45–75% for a solid name match); a weak hit reads low. It's a match score, not the model bragging "90%". Snap something the shop clearly stocks → decent %. Snap something it clearly doesn't → the good matches don't appear (or score low). honest-conf
    expect: the grinder problem is gone — no confident wrong answer.
  4. "None of these — it's a new item" → AI draft fallback. In the picker, tap the dashed "None of these — it's a new item →" button. The picker closes and the add-form fills with the AI's read (name/category/description) + keeps your photo — the old Snap & fill behaviour, now as the fallback. fallback
  5. Pick a FourTwenty (supplier) match → fills the add-form from it. Snap again, and this time tap a row under "From FourTwenty". The add-form fills from that supplier record (name, description, photo, price, supplier auto-set to FourTwenty) — you review the price + Save. reference
  6. No matches at all → straight to the AI draft. Snap something the shop definitely doesn't carry (a random household object). With no catalog/reference hits, the picker is skipped and the form drafts directly from the AI — no dead-end. no-match
