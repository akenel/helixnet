💵 Order-to-Cash — Pam at the till

1 · Open my drawer Pam✅ walk-ready

Before the first sale, count the float into your own cash box. The drawer is yours for the shift — nobody else's money mixes in.

Open My Drawer ↗
  1. No shift open yet → Open Drawer. Count a float on the denomination grid (e.g. a few coins + notes). total adds up live as you tap; e.g. CHF 200.00 float
  2. Confirm open. drawer shows OPEN, opened-at time, your name, the opening float

2 · Ring a cash sale Pam✅ walk-ready

The bread and butter: scan, take cash, give change, hand over the receipt.

Open New Sale ↗
  1. Search/scan a couple of items into the cart. items land with price + running total
  2. Checkout → Cash → enter what the customer hands you (more than the total). change due is computed and shown
  3. Complete. receipt with items, total, payment = cash; you stay logged in
  4. Try to underpay (tender less than total). blocked — can't complete a cash sale that's short

3 · Ring a card sale Pam✅ walk-ready

Same flow, plastic instead of cash — no change, straight to receipt.

  1. New sale → add items → Checkout → Card. no tender/change step; completes straight to receipt
  2. Card sales don't touch the cash drawer. your drawer's expected cash is unchanged by this sale (only cash sales move it)

4 · The VAT is right Pam✅ walk-ready

Swiss shelf prices are gross — VAT (8.1%) is contained in the price, not added on top. The receipt has to show it correctly or the Treuhänder won't sign.

  1. On a receipt, find the VAT line. the total equals the shelf price (VAT not added on top); a VAT amount is broken out (8.1%)
  2. Sanity-check one item: VAT ≈ gross × 8.1 ÷ 108.1. e.g. CHF 10.00 gross → ~CHF 0.75 VAT contained

5 · Sign Johnny up Pam✅ walk-ready

"Want to be a member — discount and we keep your purchases on record?" 10 seconds, 18+ tick, no email or password needed.

Open Members ↗
  1. Search a name that won't exist (TEST-johnny) → Add New. form opens with a ✓ 18 or older box; Create is disabled until ticked
  2. Fill handle + Instagram, tick 18+, Create. "Welcome … credits awarded"; member appears with a Bronze badge
  3. Try once without the 18+ tick. blocked — no enrollment without the age tick

6 · Member sale — points land, tier climbs Pam✅ walk-ready

Attach the member, every CHF earns a point, and spend grows the tier (Silver +5% @ 500, Gold +10% @ 2000). Bronze pays full today; the discount kicks in on the next visit.

  1. On Johnny's card → ✅ Use for Checkout → ring items → Checkout. a blue member banner shows handle · tier · points
  2. Complete the sale, then re-search Johnny. credits went up (~1/CHF), lifetime spend grew (a real CHF figure)
  3. Push his lifetime past CHF 500 (a pricey item / agreed total), re-search, start a new sale. card now Silver; checkout shows a "Member discount (Silver 5%)" line, total 5% lower

7 · Johnny's lighter — warranty, no receipt Pam✅ walk-ready

The story that sells the whole thing: Johnny's lighter breaks, he has no receipt. Because he's a member, the shop has it on record.

  1. Look Johnny up → on his card tap 📜 View History. a modal lists his past purchases — date, items, price, receipt #, newest first
  2. Find the item he's returning. it's there — "we sold you this on … " — warranty honored without a paper receipt

8 · A treat on the house Pam🔨 building

Regulars get a freebie now and then — but the shelf still loses the stock. Free to the customer, tracked for inventory.

  1. Add an item, mark it a giveaway / on the house. its line is free; the cart total doesn't rise for it
  2. Complete the sale, then check that product's stock. stock went down by one even though it was free (inventory stays honest)

9 · A refund Pam🔨 building

Customer brings something back. The sale is reversed, stock comes home, the drawer/reports reflect it.

  1. Find a completed sale → Refund it. transaction flips to refunded; a clear confirmation
  2. Check stock + the day's numbers. refunded items return to stock; the daily total reflects the reversal

10 · Age-restricted item (CBD) Pam🔨 building

Some products are 18+. The till should make Pam confirm age before it sells — a compliance guardrail, not a nag.

  1. Add an age-restricted product to the cart. an 18+ confirm prompt appears before it can be sold
  2. Confirm, complete the sale. sells normally once age is confirmed; no prompt for non-restricted items

11 · Close my drawer Pam✅ walk-ready

End of shift: count out the cash box, the system tells you what it expects, and flags any variance over ±CHF 0.20. Your hours come from open → close.

Open My Drawer ↗
  1. (If you took cash today) record a paid-in/paid-out with a reason. it logs to the drawer's movement trail
  2. Close Drawer → count out the denominations. system shows expected vs counted + the variance; green if within ±0.20, flagged if not
  3. Read the one-page shift report. shows your hours, float, cash sales, paid in/out, expected/counted/variance, AND the itemized daily sales log (every transaction you rang)

12 · The day's numbers Pam / Felix✅ walk-ready

The Z-report: what sold today, split by VAT and by cash/card — mine vs the whole store.

  1. Dashboard → My Sales Today. shows your revenue/items/VAT (cashier-scoped), not the whole store
  2. Open the store-wide daily summary. total revenue, items, VAT, cash vs card split; exportable (Banana CSV headers)
📦 Procure-to-Pay — Ralph keeps the shelves full

13 · Low-stock alert Ralph🔨 building

A bestseller dips below its threshold. The system should surface it so Ralph reorders before it's gone.

  1. Find (or sell down) a product below its alert threshold. it shows up as low / needs reorder somewhere Ralph can see it

14 · Restock + margin Ralph🔨 building

Goods arrive. Ralph raises the stock, the cost/supplier are on record, and the margin (cost vs sale price) is visible.

  1. As ralph, raise a product's stock quantity (receive goods). stock rises; the low-stock flag clears once above threshold
  2. Check the product's cost vs price. margin is visible; supplier/cost fields hold

15 · Onboard a new product Ralph🔨 building

A new line comes in. Ralph adds it to the catalog so it's scannable at the till.

  1. As ralph, create a product: name, SKU/barcode, price, cost, VAT/age flags, supplier. saves; appears in search
  2. Switch to pam, search/scan the new product into a sale. it rings up at the price Ralph set
