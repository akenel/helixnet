## The concept, in plain words (read this first)
Big-company inventory systems (SAP, ERP, MRP) keep a **perpetual inventory** — a running count of
every unit, with reorder points and stock levels. That works for a warehouse with a hundred SKUs
that never change. It does **not** work for a kiosk or head shop with a thousand products, new
stuff weekly, half of it unbarcoded, and old stock piling up in the back. There, the count is
**wrong the moment you write it down** — and a wrong number in your books is worse than no number.

So Banco does the opposite: **zero perpetual inventory.** We don't count. We learn your shop by
**watching what sells** — the one thing that's always true, because it actually happened. Every
item is **born once** the first time you meet it (a scan, or a photo for the no-code stuff), then
known forever. The catalogue builds itself, one sale at a time. And the question MRP exists to
answer — *"what do I re-buy?"* — we answer from **velocity** (what's moving, what's dead), not from
a count nobody can keep honest.

## The script — cards + VO

**COLD OPEN (the punch)**
**CARD:** *Stop counting your inventory.*
**VO:** "If you've ever worked with a big inventory system — SAP, an ERP, an MRP — you know the
drill: count everything, track every unit, reorder points, stock levels, cycle counts. It works
great… for a warehouse. **You don't run a warehouse.**"

**THE PROBLEM**
**CARD:** *A thousand products. New stuff weekly. Half with no barcode.*
**VO:** "You run a *shop.* A kiosk, a head shop — a thousand different products, new things coming
in every week, a pile of old stock in the back you're not even sure what to do with. For *you*,
that corporate inventory system isn't just overkill. It's a **lie**."

**WHY IT'S A LIE**
**CARD:** *The count is wrong the moment you write it down.*
**VO:** "Half your stuff has no barcode. New things show up daily. Things walk off the shelf. To
keep a 'perpetual inventory' honest you'd have to count *constantly* — and nobody does. So the
system says you've got four, you've actually got one, and now it **won't even let you sell** the
thing in your customer's hand. A wrong number in your books is worse than no number at all."

**THE REFRAME**
**CARD:** *So… don't.*
**VO:** "So we asked a stupid-simple question: what if you just **didn't**? What if you stopped
counting entirely — and instead the system learned your shop by watching the one thing that's
always true: **what actually sells.**"

**HOW IT WORKS — born once**
**CARD:** *Every item is born once.*
**VO:** "Here's the counter. A customer brings something up. You scan it — or if it's got no
barcode, you snap a photo and name it. The **first** time the system sees it, it's a brand-new
product, born right there in the sale. Twenty seconds, and the sale never stops. Scan that same
thing again tomorrow? **Instant** — name, price, picture, all there. *Scan once, known forever.*
Your catalogue isn't something you build at midnight in a spreadsheet. **It builds itself, one
sale at a time, while you work.**"

**▶ LIVE INSERT — show it**
*(switch to the screen — enter a brand-new product on the spot)*
**VO:** "Let me show you. Here's something that's never been in the system…" *(walk the born-once
flow: not on file → name → price → photo → save → it's real, searchable, done.)* "…and that's it.
It's in the catalogue now, forever — and I never counted a thing."

**THE CONTRAST**
**CARD:** *MRP, reorder points, kanban — all to answer one question.*
**VO:** "Now — the old way. MRP, min/max stock, reorder points, kanban cards: all of it exists to
answer **one** question — *what do I need to re-buy?* And it answers it by tracking counts you
can't keep honest. We answer the **same** question a different way: **velocity.** What's selling
fast? What hasn't moved in a month? The sales log knows — for real, no counting. Re-buy what's
moving, clear out what's dead. Same answer. Honest data. **Zero counting.**"

**CLOSE**
**CARD:** *Built for your shop. Not a corporation's warehouse.*
**VO:** "No cycle counts. No system telling you you can't sell what's right there on your shelf.
You just sell — and it learns your shop. **That's zero perpetual inventory.** Built for the way
your shop actually works… not the way a warehouse does."

**OUTRO**
**CARD:** **BANCO** — *sell. it learns. that's the whole trick.*

## Notes
- **Length:** ~400–450 words of VO ≈ 3 min. Trim the CONTRAST section if you want it tighter to 2.
- **The live insert is the proof** — don't skip it. Telling them "it builds itself" lands 10× harder
  when they *watch* a product born in 20 seconds.
- **Credible vocabulary:** MRP / ERP / perpetual inventory / reorder point / kanban / cycle count /
  SKU are all real terms — using them right tells a shopkeeper you actually know the systems you're
  telling him to drop. *"Zero perpetual inventory"* and *"born once"* are ours — define them as you go.
- **Don't oversell:** this is *for small, messy, fast-changing shops* — not a claim that you'd run a
  car factory on it. The honesty is part of the credibility.
