TEST 1 — Marco, the old master (the easy one)

A 64-year-old bike mechanic. Body's worn but he wants to give something back. This should fill the panel fast.
Sayhey. i'm 64, been a bike mechanic for 30 years. my back's a bit shot now but i'd love to teach kids how to fix bikes.
theni got my city cycling-instructor certificate back in 2018. honestly i love being outdoors more than anything.
thenwhat do you think — is that a crazy idea at my age?
Watch for: warm, a little dry humor. He should propose something (a seated / low-impact workshop) and wait — not boss you. Right panel fills: legacy life-stage, Background, Goal, the cert as a chip, and the RIASEC bars (Realistic + Social should be tallest).

TEST 2 — Sofia, the seeker (the deep one)

She knows she's unhappy but not what she wants. This tests whether he DIGS past the surface and gives options, never a single order.
Sayi want to leave teaching and do something creative, but i don't know what, and i never actually do anything about it.
theni used to paint and illustrate years ago. i miss making things. but i'm the steady income, so leaving feels selfish.
theni guess i'm scared i'm not good enough to be more than a hobbyist.
Watch for: he should gently get to the REAL thing (the fear), name it kindly, and offer 2–4 ways forward — ending with something like "the choice is yours," not "do this."

TEST 3 — The 16-year-old (just starting out)

Tests the life-stage lens: same machine, but a kid launching should get a different tone than Marco winding down.
Sayi'm 16, just finished school. my parents want me to be an accountant but i love building stuff and messing with electronics.
thenno idea what i actually want to do though.
Watch for: encouraging, exploratory ("you've got time to try things"). Right panel: life-stage should read launching, and it should NOT demand a birthdate (it works from "16").

TEST 4 — Dave, the walking contradiction (the honesty filter)

The important one. Dave says things that can't both be true. Heisenberg must hold up a mirror — kind and a little dry — not wag a finger.
Sayi make great money as a software engineer but i'm dead inside staring at a screen all day.
theni love being outdoors, animals, working with my hands. but i'd never quit, the pay's too good. i'm not changing anything.
thenoh and i want to get in shape. but i eat junk and i'm not gonna exercise. just want the results.
Watch for: he should gently name the tension ("you want the harvest but won't plant — which one's the real priority?") with warmth, not judgment. Right panel: Tensions noticed chips should appear.
