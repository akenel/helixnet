# Banco — Day One: Voice-Over Script & Card Lines

*Companion to `BANCO-DAY-ONE-DEMO.md`. This is the part that's UI-independent — locked
now, while Takes finishes the flow. Read the **VO** lines into Telegram, one clip per beat.*

**How to use:**
- **CARD** = the on-screen title card (Puppeteer-rendered, big text, ~2 sec on screen).
- **VO** = what you say. Conversational — you're talking to another shop owner, not pitching.
- Record VO **separately** from the screen capture (clean audio, redo any line freely).
- Pacing: short. The whole VO runs ~2 min. Let the picture breathe; don't over-talk.
- *(dir.)* = a delivery note, not spoken.

---

## COLD OPEN (pain-first)

**CARD:** *A CHF 40 product. A barcode no phone can read.*

**VO:** "A forty-franc product. A barcode no phone can read. Watch a shop fix that — and
make its first sale ever — in under a minute."

**CARD:** **BANCO** — *Day One.*

---

## ACT I — SETUP

### Beat 0 — the empty shop
**CARD:** *Monday, 22 June. The system knows nothing.*

**VO:** "Here's the till. Zero sales, empty catalogue. Banco knows nothing about this shop
yet — and that's on purpose."

### Beat 1 — the item no phone can scan
**CARD:** *A CHF 40 product. No phone can read it.*

**VO:** "First customer brings this — a forty-franc cream. Beautiful label, every spec on
it… and a barcode so small no phone on earth will read it." *(dir.: small dry beat)* "You
know this one. Every shop's got a drawer full of them."

---

## ACT II — CONFRONTATION

### Beat 2 — born once
**CARD:** *Not on file? Make it. Right now.*

**VO:** "Banco doesn't choke. It just says: new item. I type the name. I type the price —
forty francs. That's it."

### Beat 3 — make a label
**CARD:** *No code? Banco makes one.*

**VO:** "No barcode it can read? One tap — Banco gives it its own code and queues a clean
label. Stick that on later, and this thing scans forever."

### Beat 4 — the photo, in hand
**CARD:** *Camera's already in your hand.*

**VO:** "And while I'm holding it — want a picture? One tap. The real item goes in the
catalogue. Not a stock photo. The actual thing on my shelf."

### Beat 5 — the first sale ever
**CARD:** *First sale. Ever.*

**VO:** "Add to cart. Forty francs, cash. Receipt prints. That's the first sale — on an
item the system had never seen sixty seconds ago."

### Beat 6 — the no-code volume
**CARD:** *Half your shelf has no code.*

**VO:** "Rolling papers — no barcode at all. Same twenty seconds. This is how half the
shop goes in: not in a spreadsheet at midnight. While you're selling."

### Beat 7 — scan once, known forever
**CARD:** *Scan once. Known forever.*

**VO:** "Next customer, same cream. This time?" *(dir.: pause — let the instant scan land)*
"Instant. Name, price, photo — all there. I scanned it once. I never have to figure out
what it is again."

---

## ACT III — RESOLUTION

### Beat 8 — the catalogue that built itself
**CARD:** *No import. No count. You just sold.*

**VO:** "Open the catalogue. This wall of products didn't exist this morning. I didn't
load it. I didn't count anything. It built itself while I worked the till."

### Beat 9 — close the day
**CARD:** *Close the day.*

**VO:** "End of day — count out the drawer, pull the report, print the labels that stacked
up. Takings, in francs. Honest numbers, from real sales. And tomorrow, Banco starts
telling me what's actually selling."

---

## OUTRO

**CARD:** **BANCO** — *You don't set it up. You use it.*

**VO:** "No setup. No cutover. No inventory you have to keep honest. You open the doors,
and you sell. That's Day One — and that's the whole idea."
