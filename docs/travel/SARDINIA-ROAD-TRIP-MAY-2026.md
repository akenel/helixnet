## CAMPER RENTAL: COMMERCIAL FLEET vs P2P

### MyCamper (P2P) -- CHEAP BUT RISKY FOR THIS TRIP

**What it is:** Airbnb for campers. Private owners rent out their personal vehicles. MyCamper handles booking + Baloise insurance. 5,000+ vehicles, 87,000+ customers.

**The problem for THIS trip:** Kevin & Kathy are Canadians, first time driving a European camper, going on a FERRY to an ISLAND. If a private owner's old engine dies in Sardinia:
- Baloise does NOT cover mechanical failure / wear and tear
- The owner has ONE vehicle -- no swap, no replacement
- You're stuck arguing on the phone from a Sardinian village
- Baloise gives you CHF 500 for 5 days rental car -- good luck finding a camper in Sardinia on short notice

**MyCamper is fine for:** Weekend trips around Switzerland, experienced camper people, short distances from home.
**MyCamper is risky for:** Ferry to an island, first-timers, 2,000km round trip, when you can't just drive home.

### RECOMMENDED: Commercial Fleet Rental

Commercial fleets run new/near-new vehicles on maintenance schedules. They have multi-country breakdown networks. Some offer replacement vehicles. That's what you're paying for.

| **Indie Campers** | Zurich (direct) | LIKELY yes, confirm | 24/7 + **replacement vehicle** (Premium) | **EUR 199** (Premium) | EUR 1,200-1,900 |

**Top pick: Indie Campers** -- three reasons:
1. **EUR 199 deductible** with Premium plan (lowest of all)
2. **Replacement vehicle included** in Premium if camper breaks down in Italy
3. **One-way rental possible:** Pick up Zurich, drop off Cagliari (Sardinia). Kevin & Kathy could FLY home from Sardinia instead of driving back. Saves 2 days.

**Runner-up: roadsurfer** -- most transparent (explicitly allows ferries), modern Mercedes vans, unlimited mileage, competitive pricing.

**IMPORTANT -- ferry damage:** ALL companies (commercial and P2P) exclude ferry damage from insurance. Scratches from loading ramps, salt spray = your liability. This is standard industry-wide. Be careful during loading/unloading.

## THINGS TO THINK ABOUT

### 1. Campsite Reservations in Sardinia
May is shoulder season but good spots fill up weekends. Book at least first night. Key areas:
- **Alghero / Stintino** (NW, near Porto Torres) -- beaches, historic town, closest to ferry arrival
- **Costa Smeralda** (NE) -- glamorous, expensive
- **Ogliastra / East Coast** -- wild, remote, stunning cliffs
- **South coast** -- Chia, Villasimius, less crowded in May

### 2. Wild Camping is BANNED
Italy officially bans wild camping with motorhomes. Sardinia enforces this more than mainland.
Use designated campsites or "aree di sosta" (motorhome parking areas).
Apps: Park4Night, Camper Contact

### 3. Gas/LPG
Fill propane/LPG before boarding ferry. LPG stations are less common in Sardinia.

### 4. Two Campers = Two Ferry Bookings
- Book both on the SAME ferry (don't assume)
- Check if group booking gives a discount

### 5. Weather (May Sardinia)
18-25 degrees C, mostly sunny. Sea ~17-19C. Pack layers for evenings.

### 6. Kevin's Portugal Flight
Which airport? Lisbon? Porto? Faro? Book early.

### 7. Communication
Swiss SIM works in Italy (EU roaming). Kevin should check Canadian plan or buy local SIM.

### 8. MAX Preparation
- Full service check (oil, tires, coolant)
- **Check ALL window seals** (THE SEAL LESSON)
- Verify MAX insurance covers Italy + ferry

### 9. Beckenried -> Genoa Route
Via Gotthard tunnel (A2) -> Milano -> A7 to Genoa. ~4.5 hours.
Gotthard can have traffic -- check SRF/TCS before departure.

### 10. Espresso Maker
Non-negotiable.
