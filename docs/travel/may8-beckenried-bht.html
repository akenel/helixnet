Where I'd push back or add — the deep stuff

1. The Bossons stay is testing the WRONG variable

Other Tigs says: test the kitchen. I say: test the partnership. Hosting is relational labor under fatigue. Day 4 of a hosted week, when the Bossons are still chatty at 11pm and you and Sylvie are wrecked — that's the moment that breaks B&Bs and marriages. The Bossons aren't testing whether the apartment works. They're testing whether you and Sylvie still like each other after a hosted week. Frame it that way before they arrive.

2. Tom doesn't actually want shares

Other Tigs treated the Tom-equity idea as a tax/structure problem. Real issue: Tom probably doesn't want equity. He wants a partnership. Likely what Tom actually wants:

  • Summer access to a Swiss apartment (he's Sicilian — Swiss summer = gold)
  • A cooking role with recognition
  • A meaningful cut of nights he works (e.g., 30% of cooking nights)
  • Co-ownership of the experience, not the asset

That's a service agreement, not equity. Cleaner, cheaper, more motivating. Ask him what he actually wants before designing a structure he'll outgrow.

3. Skip the full kitchen, but don't host an unpolished one

Other Tigs binary's it: full reno OR existing kitchen. False choice. Spend €2–3k on a tune-up: paint, hardware, deep clean, replace the worst one or two visible items, new lighting. Get to 80% looking polished without the 20k. Paying guest #2 walks in, sees a cared-for kitchen, not a brand new one. Enough. The €5k/week price assumes "intentional and curated," not "luxury new."

4. The price band needs market validation, not assumption

€5k/week for Beckenried is upper-end. Lucerne-area Airbnb runs €1.4–2.8k/week even with services. To clear €5k you need:

  • A clear avatar (wealthy Canadian retirees? Senior Swiss couples? Foodie travelers?)
  • A specific value stack the avatar will pay for
  • Proof you can deliver it (and proof comes from the Bossons review, not from the brochure)

Don't anchor at 5k yet. Anchor at "what's the smallest stack that makes economic sense for our energy investment." Could be 3.5k. Could be 6k for the right avatar with helicopter-into-Brünig add-ons. Test, don't guess.

5. The OPS LAYER is the gap nobody planned

Other Tigs missed this entirely. When a paid guest is in the apartment:

  • Who handles the 2am pipe burst when you're in Trapani?
  • Who hands keys if the guest arrives early/late?
  • Who does cleaning between stays — Sylvie? Paid help? Tom?
  • Who's actually anchoring Beckenried when you're pulled to Sicily/MAX/code?
  • What's the emergency contact tree if Sylvie gets sick mid-stay?

This is the gap that kills 9 of 10 hosted B&Bs. You need a Felix-equivalent in Beckenried — someone local on the ground 30 minutes away who's paid (or owed favors) to be the backup. Probably needs a name attached before booking #1.

6. The "why this summer" question is the real question

Other Tigs jumped to "test this summer." But: why summer 2026 vs summer 2027? What's the actual pressure?

  • If cash flow → there are faster, cleaner plays (mortgage refi, company loan, rent it cheap on Airbnb for one summer to generate the 20k while building the hosted product carefully for 2027)
  • If "the Bossons triggered it" → that's just a coincidence, not a reason
  • If "Sylvie wants to host" → great, but that's a 5-year mission, not a 6-week sprint

The hosted B&B is a 2027+ business. The summer 2026 move could simply be: transactional rent on Airbnb for 2-3 months, build the hosted product behind the scenes for a quiet 2027 launch with one or two curated guests. Removes the kitchen pressure, generates cash, and gives you a year to build the OPS LAYER right.

7. The Corrado-Palermo expansion is a 2027 conversation

Other Tigs flagged "don't build expansion before first stay." Sharper version: even mentioning Corrado's Palermo apartments in the Beckenried product dilutes both. Beckenried = Swiss alpine experience. Sicilian apartments = different product, different season, different ops. Beckenried is the laboratory. Sicily is the franchise. Build them on different timelines or you'll end up with two half-built businesses.
