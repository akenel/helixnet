# Legends FAQ — Why ask a Master? (the honest version)

*This is the straight answer, not the hype. La Piazza is free and built for the world; honesty is the point.*

---

## Is it really Heisenberg / Ada / Leonardo?
**No.** It's an AI **channeling a documented person's method and voice** — their real ideas, applied to your
question. It is not the actual person, and it has no private knowledge of them.

## Does the master know more than just "asking the AI"?
**No — it's the same engine** (a large language model). The master doesn't know *more*. So what's different?

## Then why ask a master instead of a blank AI box?
Three real reasons:
1. **Focus over mush.** A blank AI gives you the bland middle — "here are five options." A master is *forced
   through one strong worldview*, so you get a committed, sharp answer (Heisenberg → measure without
   disturbing; Ada → never overwrite, keep every version). The constraint is the value.
2. **A better question.** You don't arrive knowing the right question. The master + its suggested prompts
   **reframe your problem in their terms** — and a sharper question pulls a sharper answer out of the same engine.
   *The answer is in the question.*
3. **It lands.** "Ada taught me this" sticks and is trusted more than "the AI said this." A named teacher with
   a method makes the wisdom **land** — and it's approachable for someone who'd never prompt a blank box well.

## Can a master be wrong?
**Yes.** It can confidently invent details in-character — and the persona can make a *wrong* answer sound *more*
authoritative. So: **a perspective machine, not an oracle.** Use it to provoke, reframe, and get unstuck — not
as gospel. Never as medical, legal, or financial fact.

## Why does it cost credits?
Real compute runs for every answer. Credits stay **inside the platform** (you can't cash them out) — they're
how we keep the lights on and, one day, fund heavier creative tools for everyone.

## The bottom line
> Same AI underneath. What you're paying for is **focus, framing, and a sharper question** — a strong
> perspective beats a bland one, and a named teacher helps you ask well and makes it land. It's a **lens,
> not an oracle.** That we *say so* is exactly why you can trust it.
