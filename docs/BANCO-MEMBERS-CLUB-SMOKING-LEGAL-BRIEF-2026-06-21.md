# Banco — Members-Only Club + On-Premise Smoking: Swiss Legal Brief

*Deep-research, 2026-06-21, run adversarially. 18 sources, 80 claims, 25 verified (22 confirmed / 3 killed), almost all from PRIMARY sources: BAG/FOPH (admin.ch), Fedlex federal legislative text, **Federal Court ruling 6B_75/2012**, and official cantonal guidance (ZH, UR, TG). High confidence.*

**⚠️ This is research, not legal advice. A Swiss lawyer + cantonal authority sign-off is MANDATORY before acting on any of it. Do not treat "members-only" as a legal shield for cannabis or smoking.**

**Angel's question:** could the 25-seat cafe be private/members-only — would that change the cannabis/smoking laws, now or future?

**The answer in one line: NO. Members-only is not a loophole in Switzerland. It has been litigated (Fümoar case) and lost.**

---

## 1. CANNABIS — members-only creates ZERO carve-out

- Cannabis ≥1% THC is a narcotic under the **BetmG**. Cultivation, sale, sharing, consumption are in principle prohibited and punishable. *(BAG, verbatim, 3-0)*
- The only three exceptions: 10g personal possession, medical prescription, and the federal **pilot trials (Art. 8a BetmG)**. **No exception for clubs, associations, or non-profits.** *(3-0)*
- **No Spanish/Catalan-style private-club exemption exists in Swiss law.** Earlier club-style pilots were shut down by the FOPH in Nov 2017 for lack of legal basis. *(3-0)*
- **Plainly: a members-only cannabis club outside a pilot is illegal.**

## 2. THE SMOKING DILEMMA — two independent prongs, both catch a 25-seat cafe

The federal **Passivrauchschutzgesetz (SR 818.31, in force 1 May 2010)** bans smoking in enclosed spaces that are **publicly accessible OR a workplace for more than one person** — two *independent* triggers. *(3-0)*

**Members-only does NOT escape it — confirmed by the Federal Court:**
- **BGer 6B_75/2012** (Basel "Fümoar" case): running a restaurant-like venue as a *"Vereinslokal"/"Privatclub"* to allow otherwise-banned smoking is **unlawful circumvention (Umgehung)**. Paying a membership/entry fee is **irrelevant** if anyone willing to pay can enter (no genuine closed circle). *(3-0)*
- **The workplace prong is decisive:** the moment a venue (a) serves food/drink for payment / holds a Gastgewerbe patent → counts as *publicly accessible*, OR (b) is a workplace for **more than one** employee → the ban applies. **Employee consent CANNOT lift it.** *(3-0)*
- A 25-seat cafe with a barista (Pam) is caught on **BOTH** grounds.

**Narrow federal exceptions (and why they don't help Felix):**
- Unstaffed **Fumoir** (separate, marked, ventilated; staff only with written contractual consent).
- Designated **Raucherlokal** ≤80m² publicly-accessible area, on application, with staff consent.
- **BUT cantons can be stricter:** Zürich allows ONLY Fumoirs, NOT the ≤80m² Raucherbetrieb exception. Uri likewise. **Canton-dependent — must check the specific canton's Gastgewerbegesetz + Arbeitsinspektorat.** *(3-0)*

**The tell:** the future draft law (CanPG) had to invent an **unstaffed** consumption room *specifically to protect service personnel from passive smoke* — the legislature itself conceding you cannot make staff work in a cannabis smoke room.

## 3. CANNABIS SMOKE specifically
- Cannabis consumption is independently prohibited by the BetmG (outside pilots), so it's moot under current law.
- The **CanPG draft** expressly extends the passive-smoking law to cannabis **smoking AND vaporizing** in public/workplace enclosed spaces; minors barred from rooms where cannabis smoking/vaping is permitted. *(3-0, but DRAFT law)*

## 4. THE ONE LEGAL PATH TO ON-PREMISE CANNABIS — the pilot social club
- In the **pilot trials** (e.g. Zürich Züri Can), "social clubs" are the channel where on-premise (vor Ort) consumption by **enrolled study participants** IS permitted in a protected space. *(3-0)*
- But it's a **federally authorized scientific study**: Art. 8a BetmG authorization, capped/registered participants (≤5,000/trial), adults only, consumption tracking, mandatory reporting, time- and place-limited (enabling law sunsets ~2031). *(3-0)*
- A head shop could *in principle* become or partner with an authorized pilot outlet — but only inside this reporting-intensive framework, **not** a private club of its own design.

## 5. FUTURE LAW (CanPG) — still no members-only shortcut
- The draft is a **strictly regulated take-home RETAIL model** (cultivation/manufacture/trade), NOT a consumption-lounge or members-club model. *(3-0)*
- Any outlet — incl. an NGO, Verein, or Zürich-style "Social Club" — needs a **cantonal concession (Konzession)** under largely the same conditions as any commercial applicant. **Members-only is NOT a standalone exemption.** *(3-0)*
- It *does* allow a licensed outlet to additionally run an **unstaffed Konsumraum (Art. 43)** — no alcohol, no minors, must satisfy passive-smoke/employee-protection law, operator must prove compliance. *(3-0)*
- **Draft / consultation, NOT enacted law.** (A claim about its exact procedural milestone was REFUTED 0-3 — don't cite a specific date.)

## 6. VAT for a members-only Verein — UNVERIFIED (low confidence)
- The run produced **no primary-source-confirmed** claim. General principle: genuine non-commercial membership contributions may be out-of-scope, but **selling coffee/food remains a taxable supply**, and the **dine-in 8.1% / takeaway 2.6% rate keys off the supply, not membership status** — so members-only almost certainly does NOT change the cafe VAT. **MUST be confirmed by a Treuhänder / ESTV.**
