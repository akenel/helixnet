## Verdict: the thesis is evidence-backed, not a hunch

The audience and the gap are real and cited. The platform's job is to be the **anti-Etsy / anti-gig
door** for a person arriving under necessity, and the UX research gives a concrete blueprint.

## The segment & macro (confirmed, high confidence)
- **64%** of workers 50+ report workplace age discrimination (AARP PRI 2025, steady); **62%** of those
  55+ report it *during the job search*; **~1 in 5** long-term-unemployed older workers have **given up
  looking**. → For this person a traditional job is often not coming back. Business-of-one = necessity,
  not hobby.
- **Solopreneurship is the fastest-growing form of entrepreneurship across all US age cohorts**; ~29.8M
  US solopreneurs, **64% over age 45** (TIAA 2024 + US Census). 50-yr-old founders ~2× as likely to
  succeed as 30-yr-olds (Azoulay/MIT-NBER).
- **They arrive under "push" pressure** (unemployment, can't collect benefits, thin retirement savings,
  second-family strain) — OECD. *Design implication: the door must show proof-it-works + a fast path to
  income, because the visitor is under real financial pressure.*
- **Enumerated barriers** (TIAA/OECD): skills gaps (business + digital), **low confidence**, outdated
  networks, financing, **age discrimination from customers**, low awareness of options. *The door must
  supply confidence, a guide, and digital scaffolding — never assume them.*
- **Dignity problem:** clients openly question older people's ability "based on age" (OECD). The platform
  can flip this — frame skill + experience as **the asset**.

## Where incumbents fail this person (our wedge)
- **Etsy = cost + unpredictability:** basic fees **more than doubled in <4 years**; Offsite Ads adds
  **12–15%/sale, mandatory & permanent over $10k/yr** → total **>20%** of a transaction (Indie Sellers
  Guild, corroborated NPR/BBC/SEC).
- **Dignity + control failure:** Etsy AI bots **suspend legitimate accounts** at random, appeals take
  **weeks–months**, blocking access to earnings. Gig reputation algorithms are **opaque** — one worker's
  score dropped to 94% with no explanation, cutting weekly invitations 15→2 (Wood & Lehdonvirta 2023,
  peer-reviewed).
- **Systematic usability gap:** many sites are hard for seniors (one of the fastest-growing web
  demographics); 65+ users are **~43% slower** (NN/g). Designing for the digitally fluent silently
  excludes the exact target.

## The door blueprint (NN/g + TIAA, confirmed)
- **No blank cold-start form.** Best sequence: **examples/role-models + a personalized road map first**,
  then reflect on motivations/strengths, then act. Staged guidance lifted task success ~50%→90% for
  older/low-literacy users (JMIR Aging 2024).
- **Plain language; define or avoid jargon** ("URL", "website", "Email Format" confused seniors). **Accept
  input in any format** (no forced hyphens/one-format fields).
- **Loudly promise no data harvest.** This cohort actively defends against it (ad blockers, deletes
  data-hungry accounts). "Try before you sign in, no phone number, one email" is a credible differentiator
  they will *notice*.
- **Accessibility is a growth lever** (medium confidence): design only for the young and older users
  leave for more accessible competitors.

## 6 recommendations for the Getting Started door

1. **Lead with "who wins here," mirroring their situation.** First line names the person under pressure,
   not a generic pitch: *"For the skilled person the job market quit on."* Recognition = the hook.
2. **Make age/experience THE asset (anti-rejection).** Explicitly flip the dignity wound: *"Your 30 years
   is the product."* (Cleopatra's anti-rejection value, applied at the door.)
3. **No blank form — examples first, then a guide.** Show proof-it-works, then conversational "tell me what
   you do" intake (we already have Cleo). Surface it AT the door, before any account.
4. **Plain words + forgiving input.** Kill "workshop_name / listing / URL." Define in place or drop. Accept
   any format. Safe floor for the 50–65 range.
5. **Put the trust promise on the door, loud:** *"Look around free. No phone number. No data selling. One
   email when you're ready."* We already do this technically — we just don't SAY it.
6. **Contrast-position ("what this is NOT"):** NOT 20%+ fees, NOT a gig race-to-the-bottom, NOT an algorithm
   that can suspend you. Lands hard with people burned by Etsy/Upwork.

## Honest caveats & open questions
- **We have NO funnel data:** nothing here says *where* the ~900 daily viewers drop off. Recommendations are
  evidence-grounded **design hypotheses to test on our own traffic** — so **instrument the funnel** too.
- **No Italy/Europe-specific solopreneur figures** surfaced (hard data is US/AARP + pan-OECD).
- **No A/B proof** that demo-before-login lifts *this exact cohort* — supported in general SaaS, test it here.
- **NN/g jargon findings studied on 65+**, target is 50–65; youngest end is more fluent — treat as a floor.
- **DO NOT cite these — killed by verifiers (0–3 / 1–2):** "22% feel pushed out," "9%→16% doubling of
  business-start intent," "web usability declines 0.8%/yr," "21M missing older entrepreneurs," two
  gig rating-asymmetry claims.
