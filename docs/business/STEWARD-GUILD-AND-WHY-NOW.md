# The Steward Guild + the Macro Why-Now

*The outside-the-box strategy: in a world handing out two things — displaced people and AI —
we point them at each other, locally. The displaced become the distribution + operator layer;
the engine + AI lets one of them do what used to take a team.*

> We point them at each other, locally."*

## 1. Why now — the chaos is the tailwind, not the threat

The macro (white-collar jobs going to AI, layoffs, war/inflation raising prices, institutional
trust cracking) doesn't break this thesis — it's the **why-now**:

- **Layoffs = cheap, skilled, hungry labor** flooding the market → the people who can deploy and run
  this are suddenly available (the steward layer, §2).
- **Money's tight → nobody can afford SAP/Abacus** → free-software-plus-a-human is the exact price a
  scared SME will say yes to.
- **Forced entrepreneurship** → downturns *create* small businesses (can't find a job → make one) →
  more makers, more shops, more town squares.
- **AI deflates the remote/knowledge work** → value runs *toward* the local, physical, relationship
  business: the tasting room, farm shop, roastery, the counter. **You can't offshore or AI-away a glass
  of wine poured by a human.** Our verticals are the AI-proof ones.
- **Trust in big institutions cracks** → the town square (La Piazza) + Swiss-native, self-hosted,
  your-own-server sovereignty become *features*, not nostalgia.

Counter-cyclical by design: cheap tools demand ↑, skilled labor cheap + available, local resilience ↑.

## 2. The core move — the Steward Guild (displaced → operators)

A solo dev can't install this in 5,000 shops. **But a laid-off IT person can install it in 20.**

- The **displaced white-collar worker** (the IT guy, the squeezed analyst, the sharp grad nobody'll
  hire) becomes a **HelixNet steward** — deploys + runs Banco / Hofladen / winery installs for local
  SMEs in their town, and earns **setup fee + hourly + a small monthly retainer.**
- **We give the steward:** free software (no license, no per-user), the **SOPs already written** (the
  ISO-9001 discipline = steward training material), the **recipes-as-procedure** playbooks, and the
  **AI brain** (vision, BYO-brain) that lets one person do team-work.
- **The steward gives us:** hands + local relationships a laptop in Sicily will never have.

This is a **franchise with no franchise fee** — a guild of operators, *armed not employed*. It solves
**our distribution problem and their income problem with the same move.** On-brand: Angel already
collects people and arms them (the Sicily tribe, Andre) — this systematizes it.

**The economics (the disruptive part):** no per-user licensing (the SAP wedge); client pays their own
**hosting** (commodity cost, their liability) *or* we host at a small markup; steward income =
**scoped setup + hourly + retainer** (NOT flat fees — protects the steward's time). Standard terms:
this is the **value-added reseller / implementation-partner** model + a **managed-service retainer (MRR)**;
"steward" is our word.

## 3. The individual on-ramp — leverage, don't jump (now urgent)

Not everyone becomes a steward. For the rest — the jobless grad, the stranded skill — it's the original
La Piazza thesis, made **urgent** by the chaos: you don't need a new career, you need to *merge an existing
skill into income*. Cleo greets, finds the leverage move, routes to a recipe, you post it, share it out.
The anti-rejection machine — *"would this have helped the Angel who walked into that room?"* — hits
different when the room is full of people the AI just made redundant.

## 4. AI as the leveler, not the reaper

The same wave taking the jobs is what lets one displaced person run a real business: vision catalogs the
stock, recipes do the expert procedure, BYO-brain keeps it cheap. **We hand the displaced the same weapon
that displaced them.**

## 6. The fork (the real decision)

**Steward-first** (arm the operators — the chaos angle argues for this) **vs tool-first** (sell the
software to SMEs directly). The why-now leans steward-first: in a downturn, distribution + a credible
human beats a product page. But tool-first is faster to a first paying customer (Felix exists today).
Likely answer: **tool-first to PROVE it (Felix/Banco), steward-first to SCALE it** — the first steward
is someone who watched Felix's install work.

## 7. Honest counterweights (be real — no vanity)

- **Scared SMEs spend LESS, not more.** Downturn makes the "366 excuses" *worse*; risk-aversion ↑. The
  complexity-filter still rules: hunt the few in real pain, not everyone.
- **"Everyone becomes an entrepreneur/steward" is romantic.** Most won't. A handful of good stewards beats
  a hundred tire-kickers. Quality over crusade.
- **The displaced need income NOW;** learning/building takes time. The on-ramp must be fast and the first
  steward win quick — or they take the next McJob.
- **We're small.** We don't "save the jobless." We make a real, modest path for a few — and *that
  compounds.* Don't oversell scale.
- **Macro specifics (Iran/inflation) are context, not citable numbers.** Directionally they push
  local + cheap + resilient (= us). Everything beyond that is noise.

## 8. What already powers this (not invention) + what's missing

**Have:** free modular engine; SOP discipline (steward training); recipes-as-procedure (playbooks);
vision + BYO-brain (cheap AI); La Piazza (marketplace + Cleo on-ramp); the no-per-user/BYO-host pricing
instinct; the Great Escape brand. **Missing/needed:** a **steward onboarding kit** (the install playbook +
pricing template + a "first 20 shops" guide); tiered/group pricing (the maker-retailer brick); a multi-tenant
vs per-client-instance hosting decision; a fast individual on-ramp on La Piazza.
