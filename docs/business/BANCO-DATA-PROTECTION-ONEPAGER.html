How your shop's data is protected

  • A full encrypted backup runs every night at 03:00 — automatically, no one has to remember.
  • And again before every software update, so a change can always be undone.
  • Stored in three independent places — the live server, an off-site laptop, and encrypted cloud storage. Losing any one of them loses nothing.
  • Every backup is test-restored, not just saved — the system reloads each copy and checks the sales, products and line-items match, so a backup is proven, not assumed. (Latest drill: 61 sales / 5,200 products / 190 lines matched.)
  • Strong encryption (GPG AES-256). The key lives in a master-password vault — the backups are unreadable to anyone else, including the cloud provider.
  • Kept for 30 days, so you can roll back to any recent day, not just last night.

If the worst happens

🖥️ The server dies

Your latest encrypted copy is restored onto a fresh server, following a written step-by-step recovery runbook. Your catalog and sales come back.

🩹 Data gets corrupted

Roll back to the last known-good backup from the 30-day history. Because every copy is test-restored, we already know it's clean.

🫱 You decide to leave

You get a complete export of your catalog and sales. It's your data — you walk away with it, no hostage-taking.

You're never locked in

  • Your data is exportable at any time (catalog + sales), in standard formats your accountant can read.
  • A documented disaster-recovery procedure exists — the recovery isn't in one person's head, it's written down and rehearsed.
  • Straight answer to "what if the vendor disappears?" — you still hold a full copy of your own data and the runbook to restore it elsewhere. Continuity does not depend on trust alone.
⚖️ Straight talk — what this is and isn't

Banco today runs on a single, well-backed-up server, so a full server rebuild takes hours (following the runbook), not the sub-minute database restore above.
