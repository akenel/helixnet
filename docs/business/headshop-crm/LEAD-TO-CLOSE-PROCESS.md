# 🐺 Lead-to-Close — Angel's Head-Shop Process

*"The postcard is the handshake." Proven in Trapani (café/hotel/shop visits → bespoke postcards),
now systematized with a scored list, trackable cards, and Postino.*

## The order, per shop
0. **Desk triage (Postino)** — the list is scored/tiered; Postino surfaces who to look at first.
   Cheap, data-driven. It points the car — it does NOT make the decision.
1. **Scope-out visit** — drive there, walk in as a customer, buy something small (a pack of papers).
   See it with your own eyes: location, parking, the vibe, the owner, is this a fit *for you*.
2. **⚖️ The disqualification gate (the smart part) — qualify OUT first.** Decide honestly: worth it?
   No parking / owner's an ass / place is a mess / bad people → **strike them off** (Postino: stage
   `dropped`, note why). Your time is the scarce thing. Most people qualify *in*; you qualify *out*.
3. **Honest Google review** — genuine, positive if deserved. Warms the owner, makes you a verifiable
   customer, credentials the follow-up.
4. **The postcard** — the handshake, referencing the visit („war grad bei dir im Laden…"), with the
   shop photo *you took*, personalized, in the region's language. Mail it.
5. **QR → invite** — the card's QR → the shop's personalized landing → reserve an invite to the event.
6. **Work it in Postino** — visit→Contacted, postcard→Postcard Sent, scan/„Ja"→Replied → the
   conversation → Won → and the interaction log carries it beyond.

## Two-stage qualification (the core idea)
- **Data (Postino score/tier)** = who to LOOK AT first.
- **Human (the scope-out visit)** = who to actually PURSUE. The eyes decide, not the score.

## Why it works
It's the Trapani postcard playbook — walk in, feel it out, make them something bespoke — with a
scored list + trackable cards + a CRM behind it. Proven muscle + a system to scale it. Not a theory.
