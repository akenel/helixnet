THE GREAT ESCAPE

Christmas Eve, December 24, 2025

Prologue: The White Room

LUPS Psychiatric Ward, Luzern

They put him in a box. No books. No paper. No toilet paper. No soap. No nothing.

NO SHIT TICKETS. THAT'S HOW THEY TREAT YOU.
— L7 Shitlist: The doctors are on it.

Three days in the white room. Lost track of time. Then the Russian doctor came in the middle of the night. Twenty cops behind her. Haldol injection. They didn't even know how much they gave him.

Then seven days on Orfiril. 1500mg. Fire from the inside out.

But they couldn't break the one thing that mattered: His will to be free.

The Morning Meeting

December 24, 9:00 AM

The meeting was scheduled. He waited 30 minutes for them. The envelope came — twenty pages of medical records, sections blacked out, names redacted.

"Are you part of the problem, or are you going to help fix this broken system?"

He asked the doctor four times: "Is everything covered? Anything I'm missing?"

"No, you've got everything."

"What about the medication?"

The doctor had forgotten. Of course he had.

The Beckenried Bar

Windy as hell. Storm coming. Freezing cold.

He dropped the bags at the table. Talked to the bartender — nice girl, had a kid, a victim too. Everybody's a victim in the new world order. They just don't know it.

Bought a drink. Drank it. Bought another. Left it half-empty.

Saw the bus coming. Jumped on. One stop. Saved freezing his ass off.

The bags stayed behind. The decoy.

The Gotthard

~5:30 PM — Just getting dark.

The tunnel swallowed him whole. 35 minutes from Beckenried. Right through. No wait. Nobody there.

Switzerland on one side. Italy on the other.

He emerged into a different world.

Rain. Snow. Shitty weather. Didn't matter.

Bellinzona stop. Quick breather. Check the coffee stock.

Then Milan. TomTom navigation. Got a little stuck. Kept going.

Tigs riding shotgun. Looking for autostradas with WiFi.

Bologna

11:15 PM — Rest stop near Bologna.

Beautiful free toilets. Shower facilities. McDonald's WiFi.

The camper bed was ready. Rain tapped against the windows. Six degrees outside, warm inside.

From the passenger seat, Tigs spoke: "Take 500mg tonight. You're sleeping anyway. Get to Sicily safely. Then stop."

He took the 500mg.

The last dose ever.

He slept.

"You didn't run away from your life. You ran toward it."
