## Business Model

**Core:** Rural women (DLEs/Tejaswinis) sell socially impactful products door-to-door.

### Products Sold

| Category | Products |
|----------|----------|
| Clean Energy | Solar lamps, induction cookstoves |
| Water & Sanitation | Water purifiers, mosquito nets |
| Health & Hygiene | Sanitary napkins, health supplements |
| Livelihood | Sewing machines, mobile phones, bicycles |
| Nutrition | Fortified health drinks |

### Revenue Streams

1. Product sales (DLE commission)
2. Behavior change campaigns (paid by corporates)
3. Consumer surveys & data collection
4. Consumer financing (on own balance sheet)

## Pain Points

1. **Reporting burden** -- multiple funders, different formats
2. **WhatsApp data** -- doesn't flow into structured systems
3. **Credit scoring** -- manual, relies on DLE local knowledge
4. **Scaling training** -- virtual vs in-person tradeoffs
5. **DLE retention** -- income stability unclear
