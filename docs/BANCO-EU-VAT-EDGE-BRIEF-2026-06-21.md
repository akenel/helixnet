# Banco — Does the VAT Edge Travel to the EU? (Calibration Brief)

*Deep-research, 2026-06-21, run adversarially (told to calibrate, not hype). 17 sources, 63 claims, 25 verified (21 confirmed / 4 killed). Strong primary sources on the law (EUR-Lex, CJEU, EU Commission); weaker on per-country e-invoicing specifics (flagged).*

**The question Angel asked:** "Is the dine-in/takeaway VAT problem all over Europe — so a compliance-correct vertical POS is a super edge for the European community? Or am I too excited?"

**The honest one-line answer: over-excited for the EU. The law is real; the *edge* is not. This is a Switzerland-specific moat, not a European one.**

---

## WHY THE EDGE DOESN'T TRAVEL (the three air-letters-out)

### 1. The legal pattern is real — but it's a classification test, not a guaranteed rate gap
- EU Implementing Regulation **282/2011 Art. 6** defines restaurant/catering (food + "sufficient support services," services predominate) vs bare food supply (= goods). CJEU **Bog** (C-497/09 + joined) and **J.K.** (C-703/19) confirm: eat-in = service, takeaway = goods, and the customer's *choice to use the seating* is dispositive. Genuinely pan-EU. *(verified, unanimous)*
- **BUT:** the test only sorts goods-vs-services. Whether that yields a *different rate* is set **nationally**. (The claim that goods-vs-services carries a direct rate consequence was **REFUTED 0-3**.) So the legal hook does not guarantee a business-relevant rate gap anywhere.

### 2. Germany just collapsed its differential — the flagship example is dead
- 2026 standard rates: DE 19% / FR 20% / IT 22% / ES 21% / AT 20%. *(verified)*
- **Germany cuts restaurant/catering FOOD to a flat 7% from 1 Jan 2026** (Steueränderungsgesetz 2025, passed mid-Dec 2025) — applies to dine-in, takeaway AND delivery alike. **The 19%/7% eat-in-vs-takeaway food differential is GONE.** *(verified 3-0)* The thesis's best example became historical six months before this brief.
- Italy's differential was already small (both ~10%). So in **2 of 5** target markets the edge barely exists.

### 3. In the EU it's table-stakes, not a moat
- Mainstream EU POS — **Lightspeed, SumUp, orderbird, ready2order** — already do VAT-splitting + mandatory fiscalization out of the box, via cloud providers like **fiskaly** (1,000+ B2B customers). *(verified 3-0)*
- Compliance-correct VAT is the **price of entry** in the EU — the opposite of Switzerland, where it's a real open gap.

### 4. The vertical premise is UNPROVEN in the EU
- Whether head-shop/CBD POS is underserved in DE/FR/IT/ES the way it appears to be in CH: **zero surviving verified evidence.** The EU 0.3%-THC vs CH 1.0%-THC fragmentation point: also unverified. Treat "underserved EU cannabis vertical" as an untested hypothesis, NOT a finding.

---

## THE REAL EDGE THAT SURVIVES (smaller, sharper)

The moat is **not** "we do VAT right." It's **"we absorb the tedious per-country localization that's a wall for new entrants."** Every EU country is a separate engineering project — its own mandatory e-invoicing + fiscal-till regime:

| Country | Fiscal till | B2B e-invoicing |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Germany | TSE (KassenSichV, since 2020) + GoBD/DSFinV-K | receiving since 2025; sending >€800k 2027; all 2028 *(verified)* |
| France | NF525 certified caisse | issuing large/mid Sept 2026; SMEs Sept 2027; Factur-X/UBL/CII via PDPs *(verified)* |
| Italy | — | SdI / FatturaPA *(real, exact scope UNVERIFIED — over-simplified claim refuted)* |
| Spain | — | Veri*Factu / TicketBAI *(real, scope UNVERIFIED — refuted)* |
| Austria | RKSV fiscal signature | *(real, scope UNVERIFIED — refuted)* |

**"Europe" = N localization projects, not one market.** This cuts both ways: a real moat *once over the wall* in a given country; a real *cost* to clear it each time. This — not VAT correctness — is where a vertical player could build defensibility, by eating localization work incumbents find tedious.

## VERDICT & WHAT TO DO

- **Switzerland is the moat, maybe the whole defensible market.** The gap is real *because* CH is non-EU: own currency, QR-bill, Banana/Bexio, 1.0% THC regime, cafe VAT split unserved by incumbents. Win it completely.
- **"Super edge for the European community" = over-excited.** Right about the pattern, wrong about it being unclaimed. In the EU the VAT edge is claimed (by incumbents), vanishing (Germany), or unproven (the vertical).
- **If you ever expand, sequence by FISCALIZATION PAIN, not VAT gap.** Pick the country where the e-invoicing wall is highest AND the CBD scene is real, and fund it as a deliberate localization with a **local fiscal expert before any code.** Do NOT build "one EU product" — build one country at a time.
