## THE ONE-LINE VERDICT

**Banco's regulatory moat is real but its near-term market is thin, unsized, and time-gated.** The Swiss-correct wedge (cannabis-vertical × Swiss VAT/QR-bill/Banana-Bexio) is a real intersection that *no current vendor occupies* — but it's defensible on **execution and relationships (Felix/Artemis), not on a technical wall** anyone could climb. The big legalization upside is **2029–2030 at the earliest, capped by a state monopoly, and may never expand the retailer count at all.**

## 1. MARKET SIZE — honestly, we couldn't size it top-down

**The single most important finding: every third-party TAM number failed verification.**
- "97 cannabis stores in Switzerland" (RentechDigital) — **REFUTED 0-3.**
- Switzerland CBD market = USD 138M (2024) → USD 790M (2032), 31.7% CAGR (Data Bridge) — **all three REFUTED 0-3.**

Do **not** put these in a pitch deck. They don't survive scrutiny, and a Swiss investor or Felix will know it.

**What IS hard and verified — the pilot-trial footprint (the only legal adult-use retail today):**
- **7 active / 8 authorized** pilot trials, **~10,400 participants** (as of June 2025), across St. Gallen, Lausanne, Geneva/Vernier, Zurich, Basel-Landschaft, Bern/Biel/Lucerne, Basel-Stadt. *(BAG/FOPH primary source, 3-0)*
- **Zurich is the dense cluster:** Züri Can (~2,232 participants, ~750kg sold, ~88,000 transactions, ~CHF 7.5M diverted from illicit market, **20 outlets + 1 info centre** = 10 pharmacies / 10 social clubs / 1 drug-info centre) + Cannabis Research Zurich (~3,900 participants, **11 outlets** = 3 specialty stores + 8 pharmacies). *(3-0)*

**So the verified near-term SAM is dozens of outlets, not thousands.** A real bottom-up number for today's CBD/head-shop base has to be built from a primary census (Swiss commercial register / cantonal tobacco-retail registers / FOPH licensing) — that work hasn't been done and the internet won't hand it to you.

**The <1.0% THC framework is real and stable** (since 2017; ≥1% THC = prohibited narcotic; <1% = not a narcotic, "sold at nearly every tobacco store"). This is **more permissive than the EU's 0.3%** — which is the structural reason a Swiss vertical isn't interchangeable with EU or US cannabis software. *(3-0)*

## 3. THE REGULATORY GAP — why a US tool fails a Swiss accountant

Confirmed structural reasons US cannabis software can't serve a Swiss shop:
- **Metrc = US-state seed-to-sale mandate. No Swiss equivalent exists.** US tools are *architected around* a compliance regime Switzerland doesn't have — dead weight here.
- **No Swiss QR-bill (QR-Rechnung)** generation — mandatory invoicing standard in CH since 2022.
- **No Swiss VAT** handling (8.1% / 2.6% / 3.8% three-rate structure).
- **No CHF-native** ledger, **no Banana/Bexio** accounting export — the two tools Swiss SME accountants actually use.
- THC framework mismatch (US per-state limits & tracking vs. Swiss <1.0%).

A Swiss accountant's hard requirement is QR-bill + VAT + Banana/Bexio. A US tool produces none of it. **This part of the moat is real and concrete.** The caveat: it's a moat of *incumbent absence* — a motivated competitor could localize.

## 5. RISKS (the honest column)

1. **Legalization is NOT a near-term TAM expander.** The Cannabis Products Act (CanPG) is a **state-monopoly, limited-licensed-outlet** model, **referred back to subcommittee May 2026**, unlikely to take effect **before 2029–2030**, after the **Oct 2027 elections**, with a **referendum likely**. *(3-0)* And a refuted claim worth noting: "first regulated sales late 2026–2027" was **REFUTED 0-3** — that optimistic timeline is wrong.
2. **A state monopoly may CAP the outlet count.** Even *full* legalization might not produce an open retailer market — it could authorize a deliberately small number of licensed outlets. The "legalization 10×'s my TAM" thesis is **structurally unsafe.**
3. **TAM is unsized and probably small.** Since every store-count source failed, the addressable base today is unknown — and the verified footprint (pilot outlets) is dozens, not thousands.
4. **Incumbent localization is the real competitive threat — not US entry.** KLARA/Bexio already own the Swiss accounting moat. If the cannabis segment ever looks worth it, *they* can bolt on a vertical far faster than Dutchie can build a Swiss spine. Banco's defense is **speed + Felix's captured SOPs + relationship**, not a patent.

## TAM / SAM / SOM (built bottom-up, honestly)

Top-down is unavailable (all sources refuted). Bottom-up, defensible:
- **SOM (now, 12 mo):** 1 paying shop (Artemis) → a handful via Felix's network + word of mouth. **1–5 shops × CHF ~100/mo ≈ CHF 1.2k–6k ARR.** This is the real, provable near-term.
- **SAM (today's legal base):** every CBD/head shop + the ~30–40 pilot-trial outlets that need a till. **Plausibly low hundreds of shops** — but this needs a primary census to claim with a straight face. At, say, 200 shops × CHF 1,200/yr = **~CHF 240k ARR ceiling** *if you won the whole current market* (you won't).
- **TAM (post-legalization, 2029–2030+):** gated, monopoly-capped, referendum-risked. **Treat as option value, not a plan.**

**Translation:** Banco is a **lifestyle-scale, high-margin, relationship-defended niche** today — not a venture-scale TAM. That's not a reason to kill it. It's a reason to price it richly, win on stewardship, and **not bet the company on legalization arriving.**
