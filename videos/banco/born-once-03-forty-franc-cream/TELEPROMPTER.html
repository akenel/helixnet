BORN ONCE · #03 · Banco

The Forty-Franc Cream

~95s · Syd Field three acts · the "trick the big chains miss" · sell by sight, not by scan

The story (read first)

Half a head shop's goods never got a barcode — and one of them costs forty francs. The new cashier can't scan it. The old way is a sticky note and a guess. Banco's way: she taps the picture. Every product wears its own face — a photo taken when it was born once — so anyone can sell it by sight. Hook → trap → turn → why → button.

How to read it: read the VO lines aloud, easy and a little sly — this is the confident "here's the trick" tone, not a thriller. When you see ⏸ HOLD, leave just ~1 second (shorter than #02 — we learned long pauses get cut). Let the tap-the-picture beat land, then keep moving.
Act 1 — Setup & Hook
Cold open — the hook
Card
Half this shop has no barcode.
VO
"Half the things in this shop never got a barcode. Loose herb. Jars. Lighters. And a cream that costs forty francs."
The problem
Card
Now sell it.
VO
"It's Saturday. The new kid's on the till. A customer wants the forty-franc cream. She reaches for the scanner out of habit — and stops. There's nothing to scan. The factory never gave it a barcode. And on the shelf, five tubes, nearly identical. One's forty francs. One's twelve. Guess wrong, and the drawer never balances."
Act 2 — Confrontation
The old way — the trap
Card
The old way: a sticky note and a prayer.
VO
"On an old till, that cream is a price typed by hand. A sticky note on the monitor. A number she has to remember — or call the manager, again. Every unmarked item, a tiny gamble. And a head shop is full of them."
The turn — tap the picture
Card
She doesn't scan. She taps the picture.
VO
"So she doesn't scan. She opens the catalogue — and there it is, wearing its own face. A photo. The name. Forty francs."
⏸ HOLD ~1s on the photo in the catalogue, then:
VO
"She taps the picture. Into the cart. Right price, first try. No sticky note. No manager."
Act 3 — Resolution
The why — born once, given a face
Card
Born once — given a face.
VO
"Because the first time this cream was ever sold, someone gave it a face. A quick photo, at the counter. Born once. And from that moment, anyone can sell it — by sight."
The deeper point
Card
A barcode is the factory's. A picture is yours.
VO
"That's the trick the big chains miss. A barcode belongs to the factory. A picture belongs to you. Your shelf. Your goods. Your eyes."
The button
Card
Sell what you can see.
VO
"Banco doesn't make you label the world before you can sell it. You sell what you can see. Born once — known by heart."
Outro
Card
BORN ONCE — sell by sight.
