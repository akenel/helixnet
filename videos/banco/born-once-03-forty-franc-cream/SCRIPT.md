# Born Once #03 — "The Forty-Franc Cream" (script)

*Slate name was "The €40 Cream" — using **francs** because Banco is Swiss. The hero prop is
the green-cap cream tube you already filmed tonight. Target ~95s. Genre: not a thriller like
#02 — this one's a **heist-flip / "the trick the big chains miss."** Confident, a little sly.*

**Teaching point:** the **picture catalogue.** Half a head shop's goods never got a barcode —
loose herb, jars, lighters, creams. Banco lets you sell them **by sight:** every product wears
its own face (a photo taken when it's *born once*), and the cashier taps the picture. Right
price, first try, no manager.

**Why it hooks:** "A forty-franc cream with no barcode — now sell it" is a problem every shop
owner feels in their gut. And the answer is visual and satisfying: *tap the face.*

---

## Syd Field, three acts (~95s)
- **ACT 1 — Setup + Hook** (0–18s): the shop is full of things with no barcode. One of them costs 40.
- **ACT 2 — Confrontation** (18–65s): the old-till trap (sticky notes, manual price, call the manager) → the turn (she taps the picture).
- **ACT 3 — Resolution** (65–95s): why it works (born once = given a face), the deeper point, the button.

---

## The script — cards + VO

**ACT 1 · COLD OPEN — the hook**
**CARD:** *Half this shop has no barcode.*
**VO:** "Half the things in this shop never got a barcode. Loose herb. Jars. Lighters. And a cream that costs forty francs."
*(visual: the cream tube in hand; then a shelf / catalogue grid of look-alike goods)*

**ACT 1 · THE PROBLEM**
**CARD:** *Now sell it.*
**VO:** "It's Saturday. The new kid's on the till. A customer wants the forty-franc cream. She reaches for the scanner out of habit — and stops. There's nothing to scan. The factory never gave it a barcode. And on the shelf, five tubes, nearly identical. One's forty francs. One's twelve. Guess wrong, and the drawer never balances."
*(visual: hand reaching for tube; two near-identical tubes side by side)*

**ACT 2 · THE OLD WAY — the trap**
**CARD:** *The old way: a sticky note and a prayer.*
**VO:** "On an old till, that cream is a price typed by hand. A sticky note on the monitor. A number she has to remember — or call the manager, again. Every unmarked item, a tiny gamble. And a head shop is full of them."
*(visual: a "manual price" / number-pad card, or a messy sticky-note graphic)*

**ACT 2 · THE TURN — tap the picture**
**CARD:** *She doesn't scan. She taps the picture.*
**VO:** "So she doesn't scan. She opens the catalogue — and there it is, wearing its own face. A photo. The name. Forty francs. She taps the picture. Into the cart. Right price, first try. No sticky note. No manager."
*(visual: the picture catalogue — tap the cream's photo → it lands in the cart at the right price. THE money beat.)*

**ACT 3 · THE WHY — born once, given a face**
**CARD:** *Born once — given a face.*
**VO:** "Because the first time this cream was ever sold, someone gave it a face. A quick photo, at the counter. Born once. And from that moment, anyone can sell it — by sight."
*(visual: the create-with-photo moment from the walkthrough — snapping the tube)*

**ACT 3 · THE DEEPER POINT**
**CARD:** *A barcode is the factory's. A picture is yours.*
**VO:** "That's the trick the big chains miss. A barcode belongs to the factory. A picture belongs to you. Your shelf. Your goods. Your eyes."

**ACT 3 · THE BUTTON**
**CARD:** *Sell what you can see.*
**VO:** "Banco doesn't make you label the world before you can sell it. You sell what you can see. Born once — known by heart."

**OUTRO**
**CARD:** *BORN ONCE — sell by sight.*
