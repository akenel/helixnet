Here's the question that wakes up every shop owner at three in the morning: what's happening in my shop right now — when I'm not standing in it?

Felix owns Artemis. But he's almost never behind the counter — he's on the road, between a hundred other things. So how does he sleep? He opens his phone.

Thirty seconds. Today's sales. Who's on the till — that's Pam. The drawer, counted and balanced. And the cream from yesterday's delivery? Selling down, tube by tube, exactly like it should. He's two hundred kilometres away, and he can watch his shop breathing.

That little badge in the corner — PRD — tells him this is the real shop, live. Not a test, not yesterday. The numbers are now. He's not guessing, and he's not calling in to interrupt Pam mid-sale. He just knows.

That's the freedom nobody sells you with a cash register: the freedom to not be there. A shop that runs while you're gone, because the watching is built in. Felix can finally leave the shop… and still have it.

Banco is a Swiss-made point-of-sale built for the counter, not the warehouse.
