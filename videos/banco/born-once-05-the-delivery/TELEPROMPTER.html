BORN ONCE · #05 · Banco

The Delivery

~100s · Syd Field three acts · the box arrives — and you don't count it

The story (read first)

A box of the cream arrives — because the reorder Ralph set last episode asked for it. The driver wants it counted. Ralph doesn't count. He scans that it came, and walks away. The slip's "5 trays" is how many labels to print, not stock; the packaging knows the cost (box → singles). Dock to drawer, nobody counts a thing. You don't count a shop in — you sell it down.

How to read it: light, a little dry-comic — this is the relief of not doing the dreaded count. One continuous take, ~1s on the ⏸ HOLD beats. Hand it back, I drive every screen. Same machine as the last three.
Act 1 — The box
Cold open

"A box lands on the counter. Fifty tubes of the cream — the one that's been selling. The driver wants a signature. 'Fifty units,' he says. 'Count 'em.'"
The old dread
Card
The old way: count every one.
VO
"On an old till, this is the worst job in the shop. You count all fifty. You match them to the order. One's missing? Recount. The driver's tapping his foot, the line's backing up — and heaven help you if you're off by one, because now the whole system is lying."
Act 2 — Ralph doesn't count
The turn
Card
Ralph doesn't count.
VO
"Ralph doesn't count. He scans one tube — just to say 'this arrived' — and he's done. The box is in. He never typed a quantity. Because here's the thing nobody tells you: Banco doesn't track how many you have. It tracks what you sell. The shelf isn't a number in a database — it's the real shelf, in the real shop."
The slip truth
Card
"5 trays" = 5 labels. Not 5 in stock.
VO
"And that slip — the one that says 'five trays'? That's not a stock count. That's how many labels to print. Five trays, five labels. The number on the paper was never about inventory. It was about getting price tags on the shelf."
The cost magic
Card
The packaging knows the cost.
VO
"What about the money? The box cost a hundred and twenty francs for fifty. Banco does the math — that's your cost per tube, baked right in. You bought a box; it splits into singles. The margin's already there, before the first one sells."

Resolution

VO
"So the delivery that used to eat twenty minutes and start an argument? Ralph logged that it came, and walked away. No count. No reconcile. No clipboard. Dock to drawer — and nobody counted a thing."
