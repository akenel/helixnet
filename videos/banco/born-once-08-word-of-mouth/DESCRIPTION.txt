One little cream. Born on a Friday, rough and nameless. Sold. Made proper. Restocked. Watched from the road. Counted to the franc. Banco measured all of it — but there's one thing the numbers still can't tell us: did it actually work? Was it worth forty francs?

Only the people who took it home can answer that. So in Season 2, we hand them the pen.

Here's how it's going to work. They never give us an email — they never do. A first name, maybe an Instagram. That's enough. Because on their receipt is a little QR code. They scan it — no app, no password — and up comes one quick question: did it work? Ten seconds. A thumbs up. A star. Would you buy it again? Maybe a photo, or a ten-second clip. And for their honesty, they earn points — real credit toward their next visit.

Those words don't vanish into a database. They land on the cream itself. So the next person who hesitates doesn't just see a price — they see Larry. They see Sally. They see "it worked." The cream stops selling on Felix's word, and starts selling on the neighbourhood's.

We're building it right now. Season 2 of Banco — where the shop stops talking, and the neighbourhood starts.

Born once. Known forever.
