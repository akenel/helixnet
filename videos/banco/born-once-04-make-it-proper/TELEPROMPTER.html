Make It Proper

~95s · Syd Field three acts · the keystone — Pam borns it, Felix notices, Ralph raises it up

The story (read first)

Pam borns the cream on the fly — rough, fast, so the line keeps moving. It sells twice in a day (Larry, Sally). Felix, on the road, sees the velocity on his phone and texts Ralph a link to the cream's page on the Artemis website. Ralph brings the real photos + specs across by hand, verifies the barcode is really this product, sets the cost and a reorder. The rough little cream grows up. The lesson: you only polish what sells — effort follows velocity.

How to read it: warm, a little proud — this is the team raising up a product, not a thriller. One continuous take, ~1s on the ⏸ HOLD beats. Hand it back, I drive every screen. (Same machine as #03 — it worked.)

Act 2 — The relay
Felix, from the road
Card
Felix is 200 km away.

"Felix isn't in the shop. He's on the road. But he opens his phone, checks the dashboard — and there it is, selling. He knows a hit when he sees one. He also knows the entry is rough. He can't fix it from the highway, and it's not his job. So he sends Ralph one text: a link — the cream's page on the Artemis website."
Ralph makes it proper
Card
Ralph makes it proper.
VO
"Ralph opens that link. Beautiful photos, full specs, the whole story — sitting on the website, a different system, not wired to the till. So he brings it across by hand. The real photo. The details. And he checks the one thing that matters — that the barcode Pam scanned really is this product. Because sometimes it isn't. He confirms it. Then he sets the cost, and a reorder number. The rough little cream just grew up."
