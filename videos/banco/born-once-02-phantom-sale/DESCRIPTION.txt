We sold a product that didn't exist.

The sale was real. The money was real. The points were real. But two minutes later, when the cashier went to ring up another one — it was gone. Never saved. Never in the catalogue. A phantom.

This is the true story of a bug we caught on a phone, in a sandbox, before it ever touched a real shop — and killed in one line. One way to make a product let the cashier straight through. Its identical twin, the one that fires when you scan a fresh barcode, slammed the door with a silent permission error. One seal worked. The seal right next to it leaked.

That's the difference between software that's been tested and software that's been lived in.

Banco is a Swiss-made point-of-sale built for the counter, not the warehouse. Every product is born once — created the first time it's sold — then known forever. No stock counts. No spreadsheets. The till learns your shop.
