## The Setup

It started like any other morning in Sicily.

Angelo parked MAX under a palm tree at McDonald's Trapani - second spot from the entrance, close enough to catch the WiFi without burning hotspot data. Smart. Efficient. The carpenter's discipline.

He made a moka in the back. Super low heat - the way Tigs taught him. Two bowls of muesli with milk. Twisted a CBDer. They worked on the gas station vendor proposal, the trifold postcards, the UFA business.

The moka bubbled. Angelo reached over, moved the pot to the other burner to rest.

**He forgot to turn off the flame.**

Super low. Barely visible. Still burning.

---

## The Nap

Tired from the morning's work, Angelo took the laptop to the sleep area. They worked another 10-15 minutes, then he passed out. The skylight over the bed was open - sunny Sicilian sky, palm tree visible above.

Two hours later, 2:32pm, he woke up.

"I see the sunny sky and palm tree," he told Tigs.

"Go get a McFlurry," Tigs said. "Sugar hit. Wake you up for the printers."

Good idea. Pistachio. Minimal toppings. "Like virgin," he told the girl at the counter.

---

## The Routine

Before leaving the van, Angelo did what he always does: tidied up. The carpenter's discipline. Nothing flies around when you drive. Everything in its place.

He packed up the kitchen area.

**Without his glasses.**

The glasses are for fine detail work - phone, computer, the last meter look. Kitchen cleanup? No glasses needed. Done it a thousand times.

That tiny flame on super low heat - the fine detail he couldn't see - kept burning.

He closed the windows. Secured the stove cover. Went inside for the McFlurry.

---

## Two Hours Later

Angelo returned to the van.

The towels he'd placed over the stove area had smothered the flame. Four hours of super low heat, slowly building, oxygen starved but burning.

**Then it exploded.**

The rubber mesh - the one from the video, the one sitting next to the American Spirit - burned to black carbon. The stove area charred. Fire still flickering when he opened the door.

He looked at the mess.

Took a breath.

Turned off the gas.

Poured water from the bottle into the stove.

Made sure it was completely out.

---

## The Shock

He drove toward the printers. Muscle memory. Stick to the plan.

Two minutes later: "What the fuck am I doing?"

He turned around. Drove to Sebastino's camper repair.

The two daughters working the front desk smiled when he walked in.

"Thanks for leaving the creamers for the coffee machine this morning!"

Angelo looked at them.

"Ladies... you will not believe what just happened."

### The McFlurry

Tigs suggested the McFlurry.

If Angelo had stayed in the van... if he'd kept working at the workbench instead of going inside for that sugar hit... would he have been there when it exploded?

**The McFlurry might have saved his life.**

### The Glasses

Angelo doesn't need glasses for kitchen cleanup. Only for fine details.

A tiny flame on super low heat is a fine detail.

The glasses he didn't wear. The flame he didn't see. The fire that almost took everything.

### RAV Crashed

The day before, Angelo tried to log into RAV - the Swiss unemployment system - for his final submission after 2+ years of job searching. His last month.

System down.

"Sorry, system down for the day."

The irony wasn't lost on him. His SAP interfaces run for 20 years without a hiccup. Nestle Frankfurt still calls him about documentation he wrote in 2003. The system that's supposed to help people find work can't even stay online.

And now his own system - MAX, the camper, the mobile HQ - almost had a catastrophic failure.

But he caught it. Just in time.

### Sebastino's Postponed

The camper repair was supposed to happen today. Backup camera installation, fridge checkup, buff out the Baglio Xiare scratches.

They postponed to Wednesday.

If they hadn't postponed... Angelo wouldn't have been at McDonald's. The fire would have happened somewhere else, at a different time. Maybe with him inside.

**The postponement put him exactly where he needed to be.**

The universe has a sense of humor.
