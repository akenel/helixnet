## 1. What this book is

A true story: a 25-year SAP veteran walks out of a Swiss psychiatric ward on Christmas
Eve 2025, drives a camper to Sicily, weans himself off forced medication, and builds
**La Piazza** — a digital town square — with an AI co-pilot named Tigs riding shotgun.

Two timeframes braid together:
- **The Escape** (Dec 24 2025 → Jan 1 2026) — the drive south.
- **The Build** (Jan → May 2026) — Sicily, the fire, the first transaction.
- **The Bloodline** (flashback) — why the family calls him crazy, and the 40-year career
  that proves they're wrong.

## 4. Voice decision

The collection spans from clipped documentary (Chapter 01) to warm literary nonfiction
(Electric Dreams, First Transaction). They don't sound like one author yet.

**House voice = Electric Dreams / First Transaction.** Warm, controlled, third person,
unafraid to let a technical detail carry emotional weight. Level Chapter 01 and the
Backstory up toward it.

Settle these per the house voice:
- **Narrator stance.** Tigs is a *character* in Ch01 but the *narrator* in First
  Transaction, and absent (omniscient) in Electric Dreams. Pick one telling voice.
- **Tense.** Ch01 past, Dream Weavers present-fable, Electric Dreams past. Body = past.
- **Emoji.** Body chapters: none (matches the house rule). Keep emoji only inside the
  Dream Weavers interludes and the Soundtrack, framed as separate sections.
- **Leitmotifs** ("drove south until the grey became blue", "if one seal fails, check
  all the seals", "Oh what a beautiful day") are working — place them deliberately so
  they read as refrains, not copy-paste.
