## The prompt we used
> Punchy prog-folk-rock instrumental in the style of Jethro Tull / Ian Anderson — driving
> acoustic-electric guitar riff, soulful harmonica AND flute trading the lead, tambourine and
> hand-claps, light kick drum. Warm, hopeful, scrappy, underdog energy. Slow deliberate buildup
> from a simmer (like Locomotive Breath) to a flute/harmonica peak, a brief drop to guitar +
> tambourine, then a bigger peak, resolving on the riff. ~50–60 seconds, loopable. No vocals,
> no synths. ~120 BPM, major key.

*(Took ~3 takes to land it; B was the one. The full brief: `docs/business/marketing/dream-weavers-theme-brief.md`.)*

## The ethos: the story while making the story
**We picked a track made on a FREE Suno account — and we're saying so.** That's the point: this is
how a real person with no budget actually builds. Documenting the *making* — the free tools, the
3 takes, the iterations — IS the content. Build in public, scrappy and honest. The underdog who
wins (which is exactly what the theme sounds like).

## How it fits the SOP — audio is the LAST layer
The production order we've settled into:
1. **Shoot** the episode (the screen-recorded beats).
2. **Card + stitch** (intro/outro, crossfades).
3. **Voiceover** — only Ep 1 (the director's cut); Eps 2+ stay silent of voice.
4. **Audio LAST** — once you have a *feel* for the episode, pick/finalize the music. Saving it for
   last means you score to the mood you actually built, and you can **upgrade the track later**
   (free now → a Pro/better cut later) without redoing anything.

**On the videos:** Ep 1 = your voice + this theme at **~5%**. Eps 2–12 = this theme as the bed
(~30–50%, no voice). Tigs masters + seamless-loops the 60s cut and scores the whole season + the film.
