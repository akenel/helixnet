# WORLD WAR LEGO
## "The Adults Broke The World. The Kids Are Watching."
> Written by Angel Kenel & Syd Tiger Field | HELIX COMICS | March 2026

---

## STRUCTURE: Syd Field Three-Act (60-90 seconds total)

```
ACT 1: THE TABLE          ACT 2: THE ARGUMENT         ACT 3: THE BRIDGE
"Meet the toddlers"       "The buttons don't work"     "Three blocks"
─────────────────         ──────────────────────       ─────────────────
15 seconds                25 seconds                   20 seconds + end card
```

**Tone:** Dr. Strangelove meets Peanuts. No sides. No propaganda. ALL the leaders are the joke. The kid is the hero.

**Rule:** This is not political commentary. This is a mirror. Three toddlers with buttons. One kid with blocks. That's it.

---

## ACT 1: THE TABLE (15 sec)

### Scene 1A -- WIDE SHOT: The Kindergarten War Room

**What we see:**
A kindergarten classroom. Tiny chairs. Tiny table. Finger paintings on the wall. But at the center table sit three LEGO blockhead figures in military uniforms -- oversized medals, epaulets, stern faces. Each has:
- A BIG RED BUTTON in front of them
- A juice box (with straw)
- A crayon drawing taped behind their chair labeled "MY COUNTRY" (stick figures, suns, houses -- kid drawings)

The table is comically small. Their knees don't fit. One is sitting on two stacked chairs to be taller.

**The feeling:** Absurd. Powerful men in a powerless setting.

## ACT 2: THE ARGUMENT (25 sec)

### Scene 2A -- THE ACCUSATION (8 sec)

**What we see:**
General #1 STANDS UP (chair falls over). Points angrily at General #2's crayon drawing. "YOUR COUNTRY drew over the LINE!" The crayon line between their drawings is slightly crooked. That's the whole dispute. A crooked crayon line.

General #2 SLAMS juice box on table. Juice sprays everywhere. Medals get sticky.

**The feeling:** Playground fight energy. Nuclear consequences.

### Caption:
> "The border dispute was drawn in crayon. The missiles were not."

---

### Scene 2B -- THE DEAD BATTERY (8 sec)

**What we see:**
General #3 has been PRESSING HIS RED BUTTON the entire time. Click click click click. Nothing happens. He flips it over -- battery compartment open, batteries missing. He looks around embarrassed. Shakes the button. Blows into it like a Nintendo cartridge.

Generals #1 and #2 stop fighting to stare at him.

**The feeling:** The most dangerous person in the room can't even work his own weapon.

### Caption:
> "Three nuclear powers. One dead battery. The war paused for Double-A's."

---

### Scene 2C -- THE KID IN THE CORNER (9 sec)

**What we see:**
CUT TO: the corner of the room. A small LEGO kid has been there the whole time. Sitting cross-legged on the floor. Ignoring the chaos completely. Quietly stacking three blocks. Red. Blue. Yellow. Building something small and deliberate.

The generals don't notice. They're too busy arguing about crayon lines and dead batteries.

One general's juice box rolls off the table toward the kid. The kid catches it. Takes a sip. Goes back to building.

**The feeling:** The only sane person in the room is four feet tall.

### Caption:
> "Nobody noticed the kid. The kid noticed everything."

---

## ACT 3: THE BRIDGE (20 sec)

### Scene 3A -- THE REVEAL (12 sec)

**What we see:**
SILENCE. The generals have exhausted themselves. One has crayon on his face. One is holding a broken button. One is wearing a juice box stain like a second medal.

The kid STANDS UP. Walks to the table. Places the three blocks between all three crayon drawings.

It's a bridge. Three blocks. Connecting all three countries.

Red. Blue. Yellow.

The generals stare at it. Then at each other. Then back at the bridge.

```
     [MY COUNTRY]     [MY COUNTRY]     [MY COUNTRY]
          │                │                │
          └───[RED]──[BLUE]──[YELLOW]───────┘
                    THE BRIDGE
```

### Caption:
> "Three blocks. No buttons. The kid solved it before lunch."

---

### Scene 3B -- THE FREEZE (8 sec)

**What we see:**
The kid walks away. Picks up a juice box. Sits back down in the corner.

The three generals are FROZEN. Staring at three blocks. The most sophisticated military minds on earth, defeated by a bridge made of primary colors.

One general slowly puts his broken button in his pocket. Another quietly wipes the crayon off his face.

**The feeling:** Not a happy ending. Not a sad ending. A real ending. The bridge is there. Whether they walk across it is up to them.

### Caption:
> "The bridge was always three blocks away. The buttons were the detour."

## MUSIC NOTES

- ACT 1: Ominous military drum, but played on a toy xylophone
- ACT 2: Chaos -- cartoon sound effects mixed with news broadcast static
- ACT 3: Silence. Then one note. Piano. Simple. Like Satie's Gymnopedie.
- END CARD: Dylan. "How many roads must a man walk down..." (3 seconds, fade out)

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Style:** LEGO minifigure / blockhead. NOT realistic. NOT propaganda. The Lego aesthetic IS the commentary -- these are supposed to be toys, playing war.

**Camera:**
- ACT 1: Static wide shot. Let the absurdity speak.
- ACT 2: Handheld energy. Quick cuts. Playground fight.
- ACT 3: Slow push-in. Stillness. The reveal.
- END CARD: Lock off. Black.

**Color:**
- ACT 1-2: Desaturated. Military greys and greens. Only the buttons and juice boxes have color.
- ACT 3: Color returns with the blocks. Red, blue, yellow GLOW.

**Duration target:** 60-90 seconds total. Tight. No fat. Every frame earns its keep.
